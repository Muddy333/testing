Benefits and Risks of Artificial Intelligence (AI)

Learning Objectives

  • Understand what Artificial Intelligence (AI) is.
  • Identify benefits of AI in everyday life.
  • Recognise risks and challenges associated with AI.
  • Evaluate when AI can be helpful and when caution is needed.

What is AI?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is technology that enables computers to perform tasks that normally require human intelligence. AI can learn from data, recognise patterns, answer questions and make predictions.

Quick Task: In one sentence, explain AI in your own words.

Reading Activity

How AI Helps People

Artificial Intelligence is becoming part of everyday life. Streaming services use AI to recommend films and shows based on what users have watched before. Doctors can use AI systems to help identify illnesses from medical scans. In schools, AI-powered tools can support learning by providing feedback and helping students practise skills.

AI can also improve safety. Some vehicles use AI to detect hazards and assist drivers. Businesses use AI to analyse large amounts of information much faster than humans can. These benefits can save time, improve accuracy and help people make better decisions.

However, AI is not always perfect. AI systems can sometimes make mistakes because they learn from data that may be incomplete or biased. People may rely too heavily on AI without checking information. There are also concerns about privacy when AI collects large amounts of personal data.

For these reasons, people need to use AI responsibly and think critically about the information it provides.
